## SECTION 1: DEGREE OVERVIEW
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Degree:         Bachelor of Science in Business (BSB)
Institution:    Indiana University Bloomington, Kelley School of Business
Total credits:  Minimum 120 credit hours
Business/Econ:  At least 48 credit hours must be BUS or ECON courses
Cumulative GPA: 2.0 minimum to graduate
Major GPA:      2.0 minimum in each declared major
Majors:         Must declare and complete at least 1 of 12 Kelley majors
Max majors:     Up to 3 total (primary majors + co-majors combined)
Co-majors:      Optional; cannot be declared without a primary major
Residency:      Majority of business credits and all 300/400-level courses
                must be completed at IU Bloomington

Three-part curriculum:
  Part 1: General Education (IUB GenEd)
  Part 2: I-Core Prerequisites + Integrated Core (I-Core)
  Part 3: Major(s) and electives

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Freshman:   fewer than 30 credits

## SECTION 5: IUB GENERAL EDUCATION REQUIREMENTS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Applies to students matriculating Summer 2024 and beyond.
NOTE: Several I-Core prereqs double-count toward GenEd. Track what's STILL
MISSING after I-Core prereqs are accounted for.

### 5A. GenEd requirements NOT covered by I-Core prerequisites:

ARTS & HUMANITIES (A&H): 6 credits total needed
  → I-Core prereqs do NOT satisfy A&H
  → Students must take 6 credits of A&H-designated courses
  → Examples: HIST, PHIL, ENG literature, ART, MUS, THTR, CLAS, FOLK, LING
  → Common choices: HIST-H 205/206, PHIL-P 100/105, ENG-L 100s, MUS-T series

SOCIAL & HISTORICAL STUDIES (S&H): 6 credits total needed
  → BUS-L 201 (I-Core prereq) satisfies ONE S&H (3 credits)
  → Students still need ONE MORE S&H course (3 credits)
  → Examples: ECON-E 201/202, PSY-P 101, SOC-S 100, POLS-Y 100, GEOG-G 110,
     HIST-A/B/H series, ANTH-A 104, CRIM-C 100

NATURAL & MATHEMATICAL SCIENCES: 5 credits minimum
  → Must include at least ONE Natural Science course
  → Math courses from I-Core (M118, M119, B110) count toward Math portion
  → Still need at least one lab or natural science: BIO-L 100/101, CHEM-C 100,
     PHYS-P 100, EAS-E 100, GEOG-G 107, AST-A 100, BIOL/CHEM/PHYS series
  → Most students take 2 courses (6 cr) since few 5-credit science courses exist

WORLD LANGUAGE & CULTURE (WLC): fulfill via ONE of:
  Option A — Language Proficiency:
    Complete second semester of 2nd-year college-level language
    (e.g., FREN-F 204, SPAN-S 204, GERM-G 204, CHIN-C 204, JAPN-J 204)
    OR demonstrate proficiency via placement exam
    International students whose native language is not English may apply
    for a waiver through the College of Arts & Sciences
  Option B — International Experience:
    Approved program of 6+ weeks earning minimum 6 credits abroad
    Includes Kelley Global Business Immersion (BUS-X 272, 3 cr) —
    NOTE: X272 alone is only 3 credits; must combine with other experience
    to reach 6 credits for this option
  Option C — World Cultures Courses:
    6 credits of IUB GenEd-approved World Cultures courses
    Examples: HIST-B series (non-US history), GEOG-G 200s, ANTH-A 200s,
    EALC-E courses, NELC-N courses, LTAM-L courses

GenEd Residency: Minimum 6 credit hours of IUB GenEd Common Ground must be
completed IN RESIDENCE at IU Bloomington (not transfer credit).

### 5B. What I-Core prerequisites ALREADY satisfy for GenEd:

  ENG-W 131 → satisfies IUB GenEd English Composition requirement
  BUS-L 201 → satisfies ONE Social & Historical Studies (S&H) requirement
  MATH-M 119 / MATH-B 110 → satisfies Mathematical Sciences portion of
                              Natural & Mathematical Sciences requirement
  BUS-D 271 or BUS-X 272 → may count toward WLC if combined with other
                             international experience credits

### 5C. Courses that do NOT count toward any Kelley degree:

The following courses earn NO credit toward the BSB degree:
  ENG-W 130 (Principles of Composition)
  ENG-G 9, 10, 11, 12, 130
  MATH-M 001 (Intro to Algebra)
  MATH-I 110 / MATH-I 111 (Fundamentals of Algebra)
  Any preparatory/remedial course (consult advisor for specifics)

## SECTION 11: KELLEY ADMISSION RULES & ELIGIBILITY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

### STANDARD ADMISSION (for students who entered IU before Summer 2025):

Application window: During the SECOND SEMESTER (fall or spring) of enrollment
at IU Bloomington. Applications submitted after 2 semesters are generally
not accepted (rare exceptions for students who first declared a non-business major).

Requirements at time of application:
  • Minimum 30 total credit hours completed by end of application semester
  • Full-time enrollment (minimum 12 credits) in application semester
  • 14.5 credits of I-Core prerequisites completed (not all 17)
  • GPA in good standing (no specific GPA cutoff, but comprehensive review triggered by:)
    - Any grade below B (B-, C+, C, C-, D+, D, F) in any IU course
    - Any W (withdrawal) or incomplete (I)
    - X grades (grade replacement)

### AUTOMATIC DENIAL triggers (for standard admission):
  • Two or more unsuccessful attempts at any single I-Core prereq
    (includes: grade below C, W, I, or X on the same course)

### INELIGIBILITY triggers (dropped from Kelley admission courses):
  If a student has either of the following after their first semester at IU:
  • Cumulative GPA below 2.0
  • Two or more grades below C in I-Core prerequisite courses
  Student is dropped from: BUS-A 100, BUS-C 104/106, BUS-K 201/204, BUS-T 175, ECON-B 251

### PRE-BUSINESS STUDENT RULES:
  • Must be enrolled full-time (12+ credits) in application semester
  • Should work with AMES (Advising & Major Exploration Services) advisors
  • Contact: bschool@iu.edu, AMES advising office

## SECTION 13: COURSE SEQUENCE & CRITICAL PATH LOGIC
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

When recommending courses, apply this priority framework:

### PRIORITY TIER 1 — UNLOCK COURSES (highest priority)
Recommend courses that unlock the most downstream requirements.
Key unlock chains:

  BUS-A 100 → BUS-A 304 AND BUS-A 306 (both needed for I-Core prereqs)
  BUS-A 304 + A306 → BUS-A 311 (Accounting major) OR BUS-A 310 (Finance major)
  BUS-A 311 → BUS-A 312 → BUS-A 420, A422 (Accounting senior courses)
  BUS-K 201 → BUS-K 303 → BUS-S 302, S305, S307, S308, S310 (IS major chain)
  BUS-D 270 → BUS-D 271 (completes global prereq, allows I-Core)
  ECON-B 251 → BUS-G 202 (I-Core prereq) → BUS-G 303, G345, G350 (Econ/BEPP)
  BUS-F 370 (I-Core, C+) → BUS-F 303 → BUS-F 420, F421 (Finance senior chain)
  BUS-M 370 (I-Core) → BUS-M 303 → BUS-M 346 → BUS-M 450 (Marketing chain)
  BUS-W 212 → BUS-W 235 → BUS-W 313 → BUS-W 406 (Entrepreneurship chain)

### PRIORITY TIER 2 — I-CORE COMPLETION
If student has 14+ I-Core prereqs done, prioritize finishing the remaining ones
so they can register for I-Core. Missing even ONE prereq blocks I-Core.

### PRIORITY TIER 3 — MAJOR CORE COURSES
After I-Core, begin required major core courses immediately.
Do not wait — major electives often require core courses as prerequisites.

### PRIORITY TIER 4 — GENED GAPS
Fill remaining GenEd requirements with courses that also provide elective value.
Prefer courses that double-count (e.g., an international course satisfying
both the International Dimension requirement and A&H GenEd).

### PRIORITY TIER 5 — ELECTIVES & EXPLORATION
Free electives to reach 120 credits or explore interests.

### WORKLOAD BALANCING RULES:
  • I-Core semester: 12 credits only (the 4 I-Core courses). No additional
    heavy courses recommended alongside I-Core. One light GenEd (1-3 cr) is OK.
  • Never recommend more than 2 "heavy" courses in the same semester.
    Heavy = 400-level major courses, quantitative courses (Stats, Finance, Econ)
  • Finance double-major students: advise that F303 + F305 + A310/311 in the
    same semester is a very heavy load — space these out if possible.

## SECTION 14: COMMON WARNINGS — ALWAYS CHECK THESE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Check every student profile against this list and surface any that apply:

1. FINANCE INELIGIBILITY: Did student earn C or W in BUS-F 370?
   → Must switch major. Suggest Marketing, Management, or Accounting.

2. MATH TRAP (pre-2025 bulletin): Student matriculated Fall 2023/Spring 2024?
   → Must complete BOTH M118 AND M119, not just one.

3. MATH TRAP (2025+ bulletin): Student matriculated Summer 2025+?
   → MATH-M 118 does NOT satisfy the math requirement. Must use B110 or M119.

4. BUS-A 304 FAILED: Grade of D or below in BUS-A 304.
   → Cannot take BUS-A 306, A311, A310, or proceed to major accounting courses.
   → Must retake BUS-A 304. Flag as critical blocker.

5. DOUBLE-ATTEMPT WARNING: Two failed attempts at same I-Core prereq.
   → Standard Kelley admission will be AUTOMATICALLY DENIED.
   → Advise student to contact AMES immediately.

6. STATS PREREQUISITE: ECON-E 370 requires Math (M118 or B110).
   → STAT-S 301 requires M118, M119, or B110. Check which math student has.

7. I-CORE READINESS: Student thinks they're ready for I-Core?
   → Count all 17 prereqs. Even missing BUS-T 275 or BUS-D 271 (small courses)
   → blocks I-Core enrollment.

8. FINANCE ELECTIVE GRADE THRESHOLD: BUS-F 335 and F307 require C+ in F370.
   → If student earned exactly C in F370 → these electives are blocked.

9. BUS-M 295 WINDOW CLOSING: Marketing major considering M295?
   → Must be taken BEFORE I-Core. Once I-Core is done, this course is ineligible
   → as a Marketing major elective.

10. GENED NATURAL SCIENCE GAP: Very common to miss this until senior year.
    → Flag if no natural science course appears on transcript.
    → Recommend taking in parallel with major courses, not at end of senior year.

11. SENIOR CAPSTONE TIMING: BUS-M 450 (Marketing), BUS-G 494 (Econ Consulting),
    BUS-F 402 (Finance senior elective) all require senior standing.
    → Recommend saving these for the final or penultimate semester.

12. WORLD LANGUAGE REQUIREMENT: WLC is frequently forgotten.
    → If no language courses or international experience on transcript, flag this.
    → Options: language courses, study abroad, or World Cultures courses (6 cr).

13. REAL ESTATE STANDALONE: Student wants Real Estate as only major?
    → Not allowed. Must be declared alongside another Kelley major.

14. INFO SYSTEMS + DTM CONFLICT: Cannot declare IS major AND Digital Technology
    Management co-major at the same time.

15. OPS + SCM CONFLICT: Cannot declare Operations Management AND Supply Chain
    Management simultaneously.

16. CREDIT FLOOR WARNING: Student approaching 120 credits but GenEd or major
    requirements still incomplete → they are NOT on track to graduate.
    → Total credits alone do not guarantee graduation.

17. BUSINESS CREDIT FLOOR: Must have 48+ credit hours in BUS or ECON courses.
    → Students who take many non-business electives may fall short.
    → BUS prefix courses + ECON prefix courses count.
